Root Access

I earned my psychology degree at Towson University, where I discovered how understanding human behavior can be a powerful asset in the world of cybersecurity. Knowing how people think, make decisions, and slip up helps me look at security through a more human lens—especially when it comes to insider threats and user-focused design. Growing up, I dreamed of being a spy—cybersecurity feels like a modern version of that dream, giving me a chance to protect people and solve real-world puzzles every day. During my internship at Encore Technologies, I worked on building an insider threat model and analyzing vulnerability scanners, all while expanding my knowledge of Security+ principles. I've since earned certifications like the CompTIA Security+ and Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate, and have worked with tools such as SIEM, Linux, SQL, and Python. Beyond the technical side, I value critical thinking, adaptability, and clear communication—skills I’ve developed both in cybersecurity roles and on the volleyball court. When I’m not in the tech space or on the court, you’ll likely find me traveling, reading, or exploring new practices, whether they’re athletic or introspective.
